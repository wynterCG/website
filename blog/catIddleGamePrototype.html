Implementing Battle system

Aug 5, 2025 • 6–8 min read
UE5 Workflow Game Dev

This is a concise snapshot of the current battle prototype: what already works, what’s still pending, and the next small wins to land. It’s written for my dev blog in a quick-read format.

1) What’s already working

The combat slice boots from the main UI and runs a simple turn loop for testing. A lightweight battle log shows the sequence so I can trace each step without extra UI.

  • Fight entry from the main UI: pressing the button cleanly kicks off a battle sequence.
  • Automatic opening action: the first player strike triggers on turn start to validate flow, then it hands off to the opponent and back.
  • Battle log feedback: on-screen messages reflect key moments (entering, spawns-to-come, turns, hits) for fast debugging.
  • Live scene preview: the arena’s scene-capture feed is already visible in the UI and updates reliably.

2) Not implemented yet

These systems are planned but not in the prototype so far.

  • Arena population & cleanup: spawning the cat and enemy into the studio on fight start, then despawning them at the end.
  • 3D health bars: overhead bars anchored to the models and facing the capture camera.
  • Player inputs beyond “Fight”: attack/flee buttons in the main UI with proper enable/disable by turn.
  • Combat rules: proper hit/miss, dodge, crits, mitigation, and damage values driven by character stats.
  • Stat snapshot at battle start: freezing the cat’s values so mid-fight changes don’t affect the round.
  • Luck (“Paw of Fate”): flee checks, limited rerolls, and a short pre-fight dice effect.
  • Outcome flow: victory/defeat handling, rewards, return to idle, and automatic arena reset.
  • Enemy variety: pulling different opponents from data and showing the right visuals.
  • Feedback polish: basic animation/VFX/sound cues for hits, crits, spawns, and defeats.
  • Inventory & equipment visuals: showing items and reflecting them on the 3D preview.
  • Battle log polish: autoscroll, color accents, and trimming long histories.
  • State hardening: centralized transitions and safe timers to avoid duplicate triggers.

3) Next small wins

The fastest improvements that will make the slice feel complete while keeping scope tight.

  • Spawn & despawn pass: populate the arena on fight start, clear it on end, and force a capture update.
  • Overhead health bars: simple world-space widgets that update on damage and always face the capture.
  • Baseline combat checks: add simple hit/miss and damage reduction to replace placeholder damage.

Notes: UI layout target is battle log in the top-right and inventory in the bottom-right; those panels will come online after the arena population and health bars are in.
