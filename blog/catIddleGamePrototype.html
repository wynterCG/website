Building a fast hard-surface toolkit in Blender

Aug 5, 2025 • 6–8 min read
Blender Workflow Hard-surface

This is a concise breakdown of how I block out, boolean, bevel, and trim-sheet hard-surface assets so they’re fast to iterate and clean to ship. The goal is predictable shading, tidy silhouettes, and materials that read instantly in real-time.

1) Boolean without the bite

I treat cutters as first-class objects with clear naming and keep their normals consistent. When possible, I avoid co-planar overlaps and add tiny offsets to stop z-fighting. Non-destructive stacks let me revert quickly.

2) Bevel strategy

Use one bevel modifier for hero edges and another for micro-chamfers. I prefer weight/vertex-group control so I don’t chase topology if the target texel density changes later.

3) Trim-sheet first

When I know an asset family will share materials, I set up a trim-sheet up front. It keeps UVs predictable and speeds up lookdev.

4) Readability checks

I constantly flip between studio and in-engine test lighting. If it doesn’t read at thumbnail size, I simplify.
